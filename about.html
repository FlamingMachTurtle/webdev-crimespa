Findings

  1. Downtown has the highest crime concentration: Neighborhood 17 (Capitol River) consistently shows the most incidents, likely due to high foot traffic, businesses, and nightlife activity.
  2. Property crimes vastly outnumber violent crimes: Roughly 80-85% of incidents are property-related (theft, burglary, vandalism) while violent crimes make up only 15-20% of total incidents.
  3. Evening hours see the most crime: Filtering by time reveals that incidents spike between 5 PM and midnight, with a secondary peak during lunch hours (11 AM - 1 PM).
  4. Theft is the most common incident type: Simple theft and motor vehicle theft appear far more frequently than any other crime category in the database.
  5. Residential neighborhoods have lower crime rates: Areas like St. Anthony Park, Macalester-Groveland, and Highland show significantly fewer incidents compared to commercial districts.
  6. Weekends show different crime patterns: Property crimes decrease on weekends while incidents related to disturbances and assaults increase, suggesting a shift from daytime property crime to nightlife-related incidents.
